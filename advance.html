Surface 180

The Surface 180 is a fundamental trick where you rotate 180 degrees while riding on the water's surface. This maneuver serves as the foundation for many advanced rotational tricks and helps you get comfortable with changing directions while riding. It's excellent for developing balance and cable control. Keep your eyes focused on a point during the rotation and remember to keep your arms steady.

Surface 360

Building on the Surface 180, the Surface 360 involves a full rotation on the water's surface. This trick helps develop your balance and control while spinning, preparing you for aerial rotations. The key is to maintain your gaze fixed on a point during the rotation to maintain orientation. Start slow and progressively increase your rotation speed as you get more comfortable with the movement.

Hit a obstacle

Learning to hit obstacles is a crucial step in your wakeboarding journey. Before attempting any obstacle, it's essential to master your basic riding skills and be comfortable with controlling your speed and direction. Start with wider, lower features and approach them with a flat base and centered weight distribution. Keep your eyes focused on the end of the obstacle, not directly at your feet. Remember to maintain a slight bend in your knees to absorb any movements, and keep your arms steady to maintain balance. As you get more comfortable, you can progressively work your way up to higher and more challenging features. Safety is key.

Grabs Terminology

Essential wakeboarding grabs are crucial tricks to master in your riding journey. A solid grab not only looks stylish but also helps with stability and control in the air. This list covers the fundamental grabs every rider should know: Mute (front hand to toe edge between feet), Melon (front hand to heel edge between feet), Indy (back hand to toe edge between feet), Method (back hand to heel edge behind back foot), Tail (back hand grabs tail of board), Nose (front hand grabs nose of board), Stailefish (back hand to heel edge behind front foot), and Japan (front hand to toe edge in front of front foot). Each grab has its own style and difficulty level, making them perfect for adding variety to your riding.

Indie Grab

The Indie Grab is a fundamental aerial trick where you grab the toe edge of your board between your feet with your back hand while in the air off a kicker. When performing this trick, focus on getting good pop off the kicker, bend your knees to bring the board closer to you, and keep your eyes forward to spot your landing. This grab is perfect for learning board control in the air and will help you progress to more advanced aerial maneuvers. Remember to keep your approach speed consistent and maintain a balanced stance throughout the trick.

HeelSide FrontSide 180ยบ

The Heelside Frontside 180 (HS FS 180) is an essential kicker trick that combines rotation with air time. Starting with a heelside approach to the kicker, you'll rotate 180 degrees frontside while in the air, landing in switch stance. The key is to start your rotation as you leave the kicker by leading with your front shoulder and keeping your eyes focused on your landing spot. A balanced takeoff and proper body position are crucial - keep your knees bent and maintain a centered posture throughout the rotation. This trick is a perfect stepping stone for learning more advanced spins and sets the foundation for multiple rotation tricks.

ToeSide FrontSide 180ยบ

The Toeside Frontside 180 (TS FS 180) is a dynamic kicker trick where you approach on your toes and rotate 180 degrees frontside in the air, landing in switch stance. Unlike its heelside counterpart, the toeside approach gives you a more natural position to initiate the rotation. As you hit the kicker, keep your weight centered and use your front shoulder to lead the spin. Remember to spot your landing early and keep your body compact during the rotation. This trick feels more intuitive for many riders and is excellent for building confidence with rotational tricks off kickers.

Rail Terminology

Rail terminology is essential for understanding different types of rail tricks and approaches in wakeboarding. The most basic terms include: Front Lip (hitting the rail with the front of your board), Back Lip (hitting with the back of your board), 50-50 (riding the rail with your board straight and centered), Board Slide (riding the rail perpendicular to it), and Press (putting weight on either the nose or tail while on the rail). Approaches can be frontside (facing the rail) or backside (with your back to the rail). A trick's name typically combines these elements - for example, a "Frontside Boardslide" means you approach facing the rail and slide sideways. Understanding these terms is crucial for communicating with other riders and progressing your rail riding skills.

BoardSlide

The Boardslide is a fundamental rail trick where you slide sideways across the rail with your board perpendicular to it, requiring both balance and board control. Your feet should be evenly weighted with a small bend in your knees for stability. When approaching the rail, you'll need to rotate 90 degrees to get into position, with either a frontside approach (facing the rail) or backside approach (back to the rail). Keep your eyes focused on the end of the rail and maintain a steady posture throughout the slide. The trick ends with a smooth rotation back to your riding position. This trick serves as a gateway to more advanced rail maneuvers and helps build essential rail riding skills.

50/50

The 50/50 is one of the most fundamental rail tricks, where you ride straight along the rail with your board parallel to it, keeping equal weight distribution between both feet (hence the name "50/50"). When approaching, maintain a centered stance and steady speed, keeping the rail between your feet as you transfer onto it. Focus your eyes on the end of the rail, not down at your feet, and keep your knees slightly bent to maintain balance. The key is to stay relaxed and avoid stiffening up on the rail. This trick is the perfect starting point for rail riding as it teaches you proper balance, control, and rail awareness, setting the foundation for more advanced tricks.

BACKSIDE LIPSLIDE

The Backlip is a rail trick where you slide along the rail with the back edge of your board, approaching from a backside position (with your back to the rail). As you move onto the rail, lift your toes slightly to engage your back edge, keeping your weight centered between your feet. Your shoulders should stay parallel with the rail, and your eyes focused on the exit point. Maintain a slight bend in your knees throughout the slide for better balance and control. The Backlip is slightly more challenging than a Frontlip as you're riding blind to the rail, but it's an essential trick that helps develop edge control and confidence in your rail riding progression.
