HeelSide FrontSide 540º

The Heelside Frontside 540 is an advanced rotation trick where you spin 540 degrees (one and a half rotations) frontside while coming off the kicker from your heels. Start with a solid heelside approach, and as you leave the kicker, initiate the rotation by leading with your front shoulder while keeping your core tight. Handle control is critical - keep it close to your body through the first 360 degrees, then use it to control the final 180. The key is breaking down the rotation into segments: first rotation like a 360, spot momentarily, then complete the final 180 to land switch. This trick requires excellent air awareness, commitment, and proper technique as you'll be blind to your landing for part of the rotation. It's a significant milestone in kicker progression and opens the door to even bigger rotations.

ToeSide BackSide 360º

The Toeside Backside 360 is an advanced rotation trick where you approach the kicker on your toes and spin 360 degrees backside (toward your back). The toeside approach helps initiate the blind rotation as you leave the kicker. Lead with your back shoulder while keeping your core tight and handle close to your hips throughout the spin. The challenge is maintaining orientation during the blind rotation - you'll need to spot your landing by continuing to turn your head as you rotate. Breaking it down into phases helps: first 180 blind, spot midway, then complete the final 180 to ride away clean. The trick requires commitment and confidence since you're spinning blind, but the toeside approach makes the initiation feel more natural than its heelside counterpart.

HeelSide FrontSide 720º

The Heelside Frontside 720 is a high-level rotation trick where you spin two full rotations (720 degrees) frontside off a heelside approach. Starting with a strong heelside edge into the kicker, initiate the spin as you leave the lip by leading with your front shoulder while keeping your body compact. Handle control is crucial - keep it tight to your hip through the first 540 degrees, then use it to control the final 180. Breaking down the rotation helps: think of it as a 540 plus another 180. Timing is everything - you need to start the rotation early and spin fast enough to complete both rotations before landing. This trick requires exceptional air awareness, commitment, and proper technique, as you'll be blind to your landing during parts of the rotation. The HS FS 720 is a significant achievement in wakeboarding progression and demonstrates advanced control and aerial awareness.

Scarecrow

The Scarecrow is an advanced invert trick that combines a Front Roll with a Frontside 180. As you approach the kicker, you'll initiate a Front Roll, but during the forward rotation, you'll also add a 180-degree frontside spin, making it a blend of flip and rotation. The key is timing - start the Front Roll first, then add the 180 rotation as you're inverted. This combination creates a unique and stylish looking trick where your body rotates on two different axes. Landing switch adds to the trick's difficulty. While it builds on the fundamentals of a regular Front Roll, the added rotation makes it significantly more challenging and requires good aerial awareness and commitment.

CrowMobe

The Crowmobe is an advanced aerial trick that combines a Front Roll with a Frontside 360. As you approach the kicker, you'll initiate a Front Roll, but during the flip, you'll also add a 360-degree frontside rotation. The key is proper timing - start with the Front Roll motion and blend in the 360 spin while inverted. The handle positioning is crucial to control both the flip and the rotation simultaneously. This creates a unique combination of inverted and rotational movements that requires excellent air awareness and commitment. The landing is particularly challenging as you need to spot your landing while managing both the flip and spin elements. The Crowmobe is a significant milestone in wakeboarding progression and opens the door to even more complex invert-rotation combinations.

Tantrum to Blind

The Tantrum to Blind is a stylish variation of the regular Tantrum where you add a handle pass at the end of the trick to land "blind" (with the handle behind your back). Start like a regular Tantrum with a heelside approach and diagonal backflip rotation, but as you come around and spot your landing, pass the handle behind your back from your front hand to your back hand. The key is timing - the handle pass should happen late in the rotation as you're setting up to land. This landing position is more technical than a regular Tantrum as you'll be riding switch with the handle behind your back. While the flip portion remains the same as a regular Tantrum, the added handle pass component significantly increases the trick's difficulty and style factor.

Rail to Rail

Rail to Rail is an advanced obstacle technique where you jump from one rail to another while maintaining your slide. The key is proper speed and distance assessment between the features. As you reach the end of the first rail, stay centered and pop off with both feet, keeping your eyes focused on your target rail. Your trajectory should be controlled - not too high, just enough to clear the gap. The approach angle to the second rail is crucial; try to match the angle you're coming off the first rail. Maintaining your body position during the transfer is essential to land in the same slide position you started with. While intimidating at first, rail transfers add a creative dimension to your riding and allow you to link features in unique ways. Start with shorter, easier transfers and gradually progress to more challenging gaps as your confidence builds.

Land Blind Consistently

Landing blind (with the handle behind your back) is a fundamental skill that requires proper technique and practice to master consistently. The key is in the handle rotation - as you pass the handle behind your back, keep your arms straight and allow your upper body to rotate with the pass. Your eyes should stay level with the horizon, not looking down at the water. Control the handle position by keeping it close to your lower back, approximately at waist height. Many riders make the mistake of bending their arms or hunching forward, which leads to unstable blind landings. Another crucial element is maintaining a centered stance over your board - avoid leaning back or sitting in your landing. Practice this first on regular jumps or small kickers before taking it to bigger features or combining it with other tricks. Consistency comes from building muscle memory and staying relaxed through the handle pass motion.
