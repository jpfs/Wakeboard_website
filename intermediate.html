Raley

The Air Raley is a stylish trick where you launch yourself backward parallel to the water, creating a horizontal layout position in the air. Starting with a progressive edge into the wake, lean back as you hit the kicker while keeping your legs straight and hips pushed forward, creating that distinctive laid-out position. Your arms should be steady on the handle, helping to maintain control throughout the trick. The key is to spot your landing early and bring your body back to a vertical position before touching down. This trick requires good core strength and commitment, but once mastered, it's one of the most impressive-looking basic aerial tricks and serves as a foundation for many advanced variations like Raley-based spins and flips.

Back Roll

The Back Roll is a fundamental invert trick where you rotate backwards over your head axis, typically performed off a kicker. As you approach, maintain a steady speed and when you hit the kicker, lean back while pulling up with your front shoulder to initiate the rotation. Keep your eyes focused on spotting your landing and your body compact during the flip. The handle position is crucial - keep it close to your hip throughout the rotation to maintain control. Landing with your board flat and knees bent will help absorb the impact. The Back Roll is often considered one of the first inverts to learn as it's relatively intuitive and provides a good foundation for more advanced aerial tricks.

S-bend

The S-Bend is an advanced aerial trick that combines a Raley position with a back roll rotation, creating a unique S-shaped motion in the air. Starting like a regular Raley, you'll lean back off the kicker while pushing your hips forward, but then add a back roll rotation by throwing your head and shoulders back. Your body will trace an S-pattern through the air as you rotate. Timing is crucial - initiate the rotation at the peak of your Raley position, and spot your landing early to prepare for touchdown. The S-Bend requires good air awareness and control, making it a challenging but impressive trick that opens the door to more advanced combinations.

HeelSide FrontSide 360ยบ

The Heelside Frontside 360 is an advanced rotation trick where you spin a full 360 degrees frontside after taking off from your heels. The approach is heelside into the kicker, and as you leave the lip, initiate the rotation by leading with your front shoulder while keeping your body compact. The key is to spot your rotation in segments: first look over your lead shoulder, then midway through spot your landing to complete the spin. Your handle control is crucial - keep it close to your hip to maintain a clean rotation. This trick builds on the fundamentals of the HS FS 180 but requires more commitment and better air awareness, making it a significant milestone in your kicker progression.

ToeSide BackSide 180ยบ

The Toeside Backside 180 is a rotational trick where you approach the kicker on your toes and rotate 180 degrees backside (rotating toward your back) in the air, landing switch. The unique aspect of this trick is that you'll be rotating "blind" since you're spinning towards your back side. Start with a toeside approach, and as you leave the kicker, initiate the rotation by leading with your back shoulder. Keep your head turning to spot your landing as early as possible, and maintain a compact body position throughout the spin. Your handle position and control are crucial for maintaining balance during the blind rotation. This trick builds foundational skills for more advanced backside rotations.

Tantrum

The Tantrum is a backflip variation where you perform a backward rotation off the kicker with a diagonal axis, rather than a straight back roll. As you approach the kicker, you'll edge in on your heels, and when you hit the lip, pull your back hand across your body while looking over your opposite shoulder to initiate the diagonal rotation. The trick has a distinct feel as it combines both flip and spin elements. The key is maintaining steady handle control and spotting your landing early. While it might look intimidating, many riders find the Tantrum more approachable than a straight backroll due to its more natural rotational axis. This trick is often a gateway into more advanced invert variations.

Front Roll

The Front Roll is an inverted trick where you perform a forward rotation off a kicker. The approach is similar to a regular jump, but as you leave the kicker, you'll tuck your chin to your chest and initiate a forward flip by pulling your handle towards your front hip. The key is to keep your body tight through the rotation and maintain grip control. During the flip, you'll want to spot the water early to prepare for landing. While Front Rolls can feel less natural than Back Rolls for some riders, they're essential for progression and open the door to many advanced front flip variations. Landing with your board flat and knees bent will help absorb the impact smoothly.

Stay on Rails

Staying balanced on rails requires several key techniques and proper body positioning. Keep your board flat and centered on the rail, with your weight evenly distributed between both feet. Your knees should be slightly bent to act as shock absorbers and help maintain balance. Keep your shoulders parallel to the rail and your arms steady - avoid moving them excessively as this can throw off your balance. Most importantly, always look toward the end of the rail rather than down at your feet. Staying relaxed is crucial; if you stiffen up, you're more likely to fall. A common mistake is trying to correct your balance by moving your upper body - instead, make small adjustments with your feet and ankles. Speed is also important - hitting the rail too slow can make it harder to stay on, while a moderate, consistent speed helps maintain stability throughout the feature.

Transfer

The Boardslide is a fundamental rail trick where you slide sideways across the rail with your board perpendicular to it, requiring both balance and board control. Your feet should be evenly weighted with a small bend in your knees for stability. When approaching the rail, you'll need to rotate 90 degrees to get into position, with either a frontside approach (facing the rail) or backside approach (back to the rail). Keep your eyes focused on the end of the rail and maintain a steady posture throughout the slide. The trick ends with a smooth rotation back to your riding position. This trick serves as a gateway to more advanced rail maneuvers and helps build essential rail riding skills.

Presses

A Nose Press is a stylish rail trick where you shift your weight onto the nose (front) of your board while sliding along a rail or box. To execute this, approach the rail with a balanced stance, then once on the feature, gradually shift your weight forward over your front foot while slightly lifting your back foot. Keep your front arm forward and back arm back to help maintain balance in this position. Your eyes should stay focused on the end of the rail, not down at your board. The key is to maintain steady pressure on the nose without leaning too far forward, which could result in catching your edge. This trick not only looks smooth but also builds essential board control skills necessary for more advanced rail tricks and presses.

Snake Bite

A Snake Bite is a flashy variation of a nose press where you quickly shift your weight to the nose of your board while still on your approach. This causes your tail to swing out to one side, creating a snake-like whipping motion before you get on the rail into a nose press. The key is timing - start the weight shift just before you reach the rail, allowing the tail of your board to 'bite' or whip around as you transition onto the feature. Your upper body should stay controlled and centered while your lower body executes the motion. This trick adds style to a regular nose press and shows good rail control, but requires confident edge control and precise timing. Landing properly in the nose press position is crucial for completing the trick smoothly.
